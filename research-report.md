# Research Report

## Summary

This report analyzes material combinations, coatings, and manufacturing processes to optimize performance of 1/4-80 threads in dry conditions where lubrication is prohibited. Focus areas include wear resistance prioritization, galling prevention, and contamination control through debris reduction.

## Key Findings

1. **Material Combinations**: Nitronic 60 demonstrates superior wear/galling resistance (44 ksi threshold stress) compared to 304SS and copper alloys. DLC-coated Nitronic 60 provides the highest performance, followed by aluminum-bronze with DLC.
2. **Coatings**: Diamond-like carbon (DLC) with PVD underlayers reduces friction coefficients to 0.1-0.2 without outgassing. Hydrogen-free ta-C DLC variants offer superior thermal resistance (≤450°C) compared to hydrogenated types.
3. **Manufacturing**: Formed threads eliminate debris generation through cold-working, providing 20-35% higher strength than cut threads while maintaining vacuum-safe surfaces via material displacement.
4. **Failure Mechanisms**: Thermal expansion mismatches accelerate wear in threaded pairs - Nitronic 60/aluminum-bronze pairs reduce this risk compared to similar-material combinations.
5. **Cost**: Nitronic 60 with DLC coating offers 40-60% cost savings versus nickel/cobalt alloys while maintaining comparable performance. Estimated cost: $18-22/unit (material + coating).

## Ranked Material Combinations
1. **Wear Resistance**:
   - Nitronic 60 + DLC coating
   - Aluminum-bronze + DLC
   - Phosphor bronze + electropolishing
   - 304SS + Kolsterizing
   - Brass + PTFE coating

2. **Galling Prevention**:
   - Nitronic 60 (uncoated)
   - DLC-coated aluminum-bronze
   - Mismatched 17-4PH H900/H1150
   - Nitrided 5083-H111 aluminum
   - PTFE-coated brass

3. **Debris Control**:
   - Formed Nitronic 60 threads
   - Electropolished aluminum-bronze
   - Roll-formed 304SS
   - Cut phosphor bronze (with chip evacuation)
   - Brass with thread grinding

## Recommendations
1. **Primary Solution**: Use cold-formed Nitronic 60 threads with ta-C DLC coating (2-5μm) over PVD Ti underlayer. Pair with electropolished aluminum-bronze inserts.
2. **Alternative**: Aluminum-bronze with DLC coating + rolled threads for cost-sensitive applications requiring moderate thermal stability (≤350°C).
3. **Process Optimization**:
   - Maintain 20 Rc minimum hardness difference between thread pairs
   - Apply 15-20% reduced torque for formed threads
   - Implement argon-shielded PACVD coating process to prevent oxidation

## Economic Analysis
Best-performing combination (Nitronic 60 + DLC):
- Material cost: $12-15/unit
- Coating/PVD processing: $6-7/unit
- Total: $18-22/unit

This represents 30-50% lower lifecycle costs versus nickel alloys ($35-45/unit) due to reduced maintenance and 3-5x longer service intervals.
